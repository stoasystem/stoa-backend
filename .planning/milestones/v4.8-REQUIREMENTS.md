# Requirements: v4.8 Support Provider Expansion And CRM Automation

**Milestone:** v4.8
**Status:** Active planning
**Created:** 2026-06-12

## Goal

Expand the v4.5 support handoff foundation beyond the controlled `internal_queue` path into approved third-party provider adapters, delivery retry workers, two-way ticket synchronization, support SLA analytics, and controlled CRM/customer messaging automation.

This is an internal development milestone. Prioritize feature construction and operable support workflows. Keep safety checks focused on support evidence boundaries, destination approval, and customer-message controls; do not spend this milestone on broad unrelated security/compliance testing.

## Requirements

### SUPPORTPROV-01 Support Provider Expansion Contract And Adapter Readiness

Implementers have a concrete provider expansion contract before adding third-party support writes or CRM automation.

Acceptance criteria:

- Contract identifies approved destination modes, adapter ownership, credential injection path, readiness states, provider payload shape, and refusal behavior for unapproved destinations.
- Contract preserves v4.5 metadata-only support evidence boundaries and defines which fields may be sent to external support or CRM systems.
- Contract defines ticket lifecycle vocabulary, correlation identifiers, dedupe keys, idempotency behavior, retry eligibility, and two-way sync conflict rules.
- Contract defines support SLA analytics inputs, aggregation windows, status vocabulary, and admin visibility requirements.
- Contract defines controlled customer-message templates, operator approval points, opt-out/failure states, and audit evidence.

### SUPPORTPROV-02 Approved Third-Party Support Adapter And Delivery Worker

Support evidence packages can be delivered to an approved support provider through a controlled adapter and worker path.

Acceptance criteria:

- Backend exposes configuration/readiness behavior for at least one approved third-party adapter mode while continuing to support `internal_queue`.
- Delivery worker can create a provider ticket or equivalent support case from a support-safe evidence package.
- Delivery persists provider ticket IDs, delivery attempts, lifecycle status, request correlation, and redacted result metadata.
- Provider writes fail closed when credentials, destination approval, payload validation, or provider readiness are missing.
- Tests cover internal queue continuity, approved provider success, missing credentials, unapproved destination refusal, provider failure, and duplicate/idempotent delivery.

### SUPPORTPROV-03 Retry Workers And Two-Way Ticket Synchronization

Operators can retry failed support deliveries and keep STOA support state synchronized with provider ticket state.

Acceptance criteria:

- Failed provider deliveries are retryable through bounded worker behavior with attempt limits, backoff metadata, and operator-visible status.
- Two-way sync ingests provider ticket status, assignee/priority tags where available, resolution state, and provider update timestamps without importing raw private payloads.
- Sync uses stable correlation keys and dedupe rules to avoid duplicate tickets or stale status overwrites.
- Admin support queue/detail views show retry eligibility, sync freshness, provider status, and conflict markers.
- Tests cover retry success, exhausted retry, provider status update, stale provider update refusal, duplicate webhook/poll handling, and sync conflict reporting.

### SUPPORTPROV-04 Support SLA Analytics And Controlled CRM Messaging

Support operations can monitor SLA performance and send controlled customer/support messages from approved support events.

Acceptance criteria:

- Backend computes support SLA metrics for queued, delivered, acknowledged, first response, resolved, failed, and reopened states.
- Admin analytics expose aggregate SLA status, overdue queues, provider failure rates, retry backlog, and customer-message outcomes.
- Controlled CRM/customer messaging supports approved templates for support receipt, status update, resolution, and escalation messages.
- Messaging requires destination/template approval and persists send/refusal/failure evidence with correlation to the support ticket.
- Tests cover SLA aggregation, overdue classification, provider failure analytics, approved template send, opt-out/refusal, and messaging provider failure.

### VERIFY-31 v4.8 Support Provider Release Gate And Operations Audit

v4.8 closes with support-provider release evidence and updated remaining-feature planning.

Acceptance criteria:

- Focused backend tests and relevant static checks pass or isolate documented pre-existing failures.
- Adapter readiness, provider delivery, retry worker, two-way sync, SLA analytics, and controlled messaging behavior are verified.
- Requirements, roadmap, state, feature gap docs, and remaining-feature queue reflect completed v4.8 work.
- Final audit records provider activation state: internal-only, provider-ready, provider-enabled, blocked, or deferred.
- Next milestone recommendation is updated from the remaining feature queue.

## Future Requirements

- Production notification and native delivery rollout.
- Final live payment activation operations once external provider prerequisites are ready.
- Rich curriculum editor UI and production content migration.
- Native mobile app rollout and full localization governance.
- Long-term adaptive sequencing, autonomous tutoring, and warehouse-backed analytics.

## Out of Scope

- Unapproved third-party support or CRM writes.
- Broad marketing campaign automation.
- Importing raw private report artifacts, raw report JSON/HTML, presigned URLs, auth tokens, or raw provider payloads into support/CRM surfaces.
- Replacing the support provider's own ticketing workflow.
- Final payment activation or production notification rollout.
