# Roadmap: v5.18 Warehouse BI Observability And Product Analytics Activation

**Status:** Planned
**Created:** 2026-07-05
**Prior milestone:** v5.17 External Provider Activation Smoke And Release Operations

## Goal

Activate operational analytics after product semantics and provider readiness are explicit: warehouse/BI export jobs, aggregate dashboards, support-safe observability, APM/alert routing, and operator analytics runbooks.

## Why This Follows v5.17

v5.2 and later milestones created warehouse-ready analytics schemas and dashboard contracts, while v5.15 made usage/quota semantics support-safe. v5.18 should not precede provider/release operations because analytics without clear activation state will mix local-ready, live-ready, and externally blocked events. After v5.17 classifies provider activation, BI/APM can use clean dimensions.

## Product Purpose

- Operators can see usage, billing readiness, curriculum migration, teacher help, notification, and support health trends.
- Support and product teams can diagnose issues without raw learning content or provider payloads.
- Release operations produce metrics and alerts instead of one-off manual evidence.

## Workstreams

1. Analytics reality audit and event taxonomy refresh.
2. Warehouse export job activation and aggregate schema validation.
3. Operator dashboards for usage, billing, curriculum, teacher help, notifications, and support.
4. APM/alert routing with low-cardinality support-safe errors.
5. BI/observability release gate and runbooks.

## Acceptance

- Warehouse exports use aggregate/support-safe data only.
- Dashboard dimensions distinguish live, blocked, read-only, safe-fixture, and local-only states.
- Alerting avoids raw content, secrets, provider payloads, and high-cardinality private values.
- Backfill/retry/idempotency behavior is documented and tested.
- Operators receive a runbook for dashboard interpretation and incident triage.

## Out of Scope

- New product features unrelated to observability.
- Raw learning analytics warehouse containing private prompts, answers, or provider payloads.
- Native/mobile implementation.
