# Plan 472-03: Public privilege barrier and canonical teacher terminology

<objective>
Make public account creation structurally incapable of privileged provisioning and remove `tutor` as an active STOA role/API/runtime contract without retaining an alias.
</objective>

## Artifacts this phase produces

- Exact public registration role allowlist validated before any provider/repository access.
- Canonical teacher endpoints/models/services under `/teachers`; deletion of `routers/tutors.py` and `/tutors` registration.
- Renamed `tests/test_teacher_availability.py` replacing `tests/test_tutor_availability.py`.
- Semantic executable terminology gate over active source, tests, scripts, and mobile/API contracts, with an exact machine-readable allowlist limited to negative-input and historical-reconciliation fixtures.

## Locked decision coverage

- D-22: `teacher` is the sole term; `tutor` is removed/rejected with no alias.

<threat_model>
Assets: privileged Cognito groups/profiles and teacher-only APIs. Trust boundaries: public payload to auth router; legacy route/value to canonical runtime. Abuse cases: caller submits admin/teacher/tutor, case/Unicode/plural/whitespace variant; confirmation/refresh later promotes a rejected role; hidden alias keeps old privilege path reachable. Mitigations: exact Pydantic/domain allowlist before provider construction, confirmation bound to already-approved non-privileged command, no caller-selected privileged client, complete removal of tutor route/value/normalization, zero-mutation recorders and active-tree scan. Validation: T-472-01 and T-472-03.
</threat_model>
