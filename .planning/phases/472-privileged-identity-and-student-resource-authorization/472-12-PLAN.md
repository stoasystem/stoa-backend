# Plan 472-12: Conflict-wide capability quarantine and non-revival proof

<objective>
Close G-02 by revoking every current grant attached to any non-exact privileged identity through a concrete conditional adapter, and make historical grant revival impossible without a distinct approved grant command and later lineage generation.
</objective>

## Artifacts this phase produces

- `src/stoa/security/reconciliation.py`: `RepositoryTighteningAdapter`, connecting deterministic `remove_grant` actions to conditional repository revocation and idempotent audit evidence.

<must_haves>
Truths: every non-exact identity has zero effective grants, restore cannot revive history, and replacement authority requires a new manager-approved grant identity at a later generation. Artifact: the concrete repository-backed adapter. Key link: reconciliation advances the exact current pointer read by fresh Actor resolution.
</must_haves>

<threat_model>
Assets: privileged identities, current grant pointers, immutable grant revisions, manager commands, and reconciliation checkpoints. Trust boundaries: provider/profile/binding/approval inventory to classification; action expected state to DynamoDB; account restore and later regrant to fresh Actor grants. Threats: a valid grant survives conflict, a historical row revives, a stale action revokes a replacement, partial replay loses audit, or automation issues authority. Mitigations: all-grant removal for every non-exact classification; one conditional current pointer per user/capability/scope lineage; append-only generation/version rows; deterministic action IDs; mutation-free restore conflict; and explicit `admin_identity_manager` approval for a new command/grant identity. Verification covers all classifications, active/revoked legacy migration, duplicate legacy rows, stale expected state, revoke-before-audit failure, replay, restore, and regrant. Residual risk: provider cleanup may fail after local suspension, but local current status/pointer deny. Phase 475 owns unrelated relationship/takeover transactions, not this plan's conditional grant transition; Phase 474 owns unrelated Settings fixtures. No phase-owned High/Critical threat may remain unresolved.
</threat_model>
