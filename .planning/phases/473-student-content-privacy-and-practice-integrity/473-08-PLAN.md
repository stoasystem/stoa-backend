# Plan 473-08: Opaque upload gateway and immutable-byte promotion

<objective>
Close CR-001, WR-001, and WR-004 by replacing direct S3 presigned POST disclosure with an authenticated chunk gateway and promoting the exact validated bytes to a new server-only immutable object before any OCR, extraction, or durable association.
</objective>

## Artifacts this phase produces

- `UploadIntentResponse` with only `uploadId`, `expiresAt`, `maxBytes`, `chunkBytes`, `acceptedTypes`, and `status`; no `url` or `fields`.
- `POST /files/intents`, `PUT /files/{upload_id}/chunks/{part_number}`, and `POST /files/{upload_id}/complete` Actor-owned gateway contracts; the old `/files/presign` and `/files/{upload_id}/finalize` routes do not exist.
- `UploadPart` ledger rows claimed as `uploading` before provider mutation, containing logical index, canonical SHA-256, byte length, lease/fencing state, and later private provider ETag/checksum, with same-checksum wait/replay and split-failure reconciliation.
- Repository transitions for `issuing -> pending_upload -> assembling -> validating -> validated`, terminal invalidation, multipart abort, and immutable promotion metadata.
- `promote_validated_bytes` storing `immutable_object_key`, `immutable_version_id`, `immutable_etag`, and `content_sha256` privately; transient staging coordinates never enter durable attachment rows.
- A bounded `SpooledTemporaryFile` pipeline with a 1 MiB memory threshold, 5 MiB part ceiling, 50 MiB total ceiling plus one sentinel byte, incremental length/SHA-256, seekable validation, and rewinded multipart promotion.
- Version/checksum-bound S3 reads and Rekognition `S3Object.Version` calls.
- Version-aware release, purge, last-reference finalize, expired cleanup, multipart abort, and final-object cleanup operations.
- Same-length replacement, concurrent chunk/complete, issuance failure, and actual-coordinate canary tests.

<threat_model>
Assets are private upload bytes, OCR/extracted text, storage coordinates, intent/part state, quota, and question/conversation associations. TOCTOU threats include same-length staging replacement during or after validation and concurrent completion; the mitigation is validation from one bounded seekable spool followed by promotion of that same stream to a fresh server-only key, recording version and SHA-256 before status becomes `validated`. Replay and concurrent-write threats include synchronized different-byte writes to one part number, lost chunk responses, crash after part claim, provider success before ledger finalize, out-of-order upload, second completion, and later reuse of staging coordinates. A conditional checksum-bound `uploading` claim occurs before every `UploadPart`; a different checksum cannot reach the provider, while same-checksum callers wait or reconcile under a bounded fenced lease. Memory/disk exhaustion is bounded by a 1 MiB memory spool, exact 5 MiB non-final parts, declared final-part length, aggregate 50 MiB ceiling, and max-plus-one reads. Log-disclosure threats are tested with staging/final key, provider, OCR, and content canaries. Error-oracle threats are controlled by owner resolution and the closed attachment error contract. Quota/ref double effects remain behind conditional durable-association transactions and version-aware deletion tests. Provider failure at intent persistence, multipart initiation/upload/complete, promotion, abort, or delete maps to category-only `upload_service_unavailable`; exhausted part reconciliation terminally aborts the multipart upload, and exact-version cleanup prevents a usable orphan or deletion of a newer version. Residual external risk is actual bucket versioning/policy behavior, which remains honestly NOT RUN until approved non-production S3 evidence exists.
</threat_model>
