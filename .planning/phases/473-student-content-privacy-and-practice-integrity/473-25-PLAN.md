# Plan 473-25: Immutable practice attempts and enforceable non-derivable hints

<objective>
Bind answer-bearing results to immutable durable attempt snapshots, make challenge identity direct/unique/versioned, and replace free-form/substring-based hint safety with an enforceable closed non-derivable template policy while preserving answer-free previews per D-18 through D-20 and D-22.

Purpose: Ensure pre-attempt answer secrecy and post-attempt result stability even when curriculum content changes or identifiers collide.
Output: Direct challenge resolution, immutable attempt receipts, closed hint templates/decisions, and recursive route tests.
</objective>

## Artifacts this phase produces

- Wave-0 tests for edit/delete/ID-reuse, duplicate lookup, semantic hint adversaries, and preview canaries.
- Pointer-only direct challenge lookup rows and duplicate-refusing seed behavior.
- Snapshot-only answer results and parameter-free content-hash-bound non-derivable hint decisions.

<must_haves>
Truths: no answer without a durable receipt; result content is immutable; hint output cannot be influenced by answer-bearing fields even when approval provenance is valid; previews leak no answer key/value. Artifacts: lookup pointer, attempt snapshot, closed template/decision policy, seed gates, and tests. Key links: challenge version to receipt, receipt to result, and a safe template decision to constant hint output.
</must_haves>

<threat_model>
ASVS L1 assets are correct answers, explanations, feedback, hint output, challenge versions, attempt receipts, and student preview schemas. Trust boundaries are seeded content to direct lookup, current challenge to immutable receipt, receipt to answer-bearing projection, and reviewer/policy records to pre-attempt hint output. High-severity blockers are scan-first duplicate challenge resolution, answer from current edited/deleted content, ID reuse changing a result, result without durable receipt, approval provenance being treated as semantic authorization, validly approved answer paraphrases/translations/reconstructions/choice cues/encodings/explanation steps, stale decisions, recursive key/value leakage, or raw private canary. Mitigate with pointer-only direct keys, version/content hashes, conditional immutable attempts, snapshot-only projections, a closed parameter-free template catalog, an explicit fail-closed non-derivable policy decision, whole-content hash revalidation, and route-level recursive canary tests. Reviewer provenance is necessary evidence but never sufficient authorization.
</threat_model>
