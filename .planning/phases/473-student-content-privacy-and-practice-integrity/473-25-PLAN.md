# Plan 473-25: Immutable practice attempts and content-bound hint approval

<objective>
Bind answer-bearing results to immutable durable attempt snapshots, make challenge identity direct/unique/versioned, and replace substring hint safety with governed content-bound approval while preserving answer-free previews per D-18 through D-20 and D-22.

Purpose: Ensure pre-attempt answer secrecy and post-attempt result stability even when curriculum content changes or identifiers collide.
Output: Direct challenge resolution, immutable attempt receipts, approved hint provenance, and recursive route tests.
</objective>

## Artifacts this phase produces

- Wave-0 tests for edit/delete/ID-reuse, duplicate lookup, semantic hint adversaries, and preview canaries.
- Pointer-only direct challenge lookup rows and duplicate-refusing seed behavior.
- Snapshot-only answer results and content-hash-bound hint approvals.

<must_haves>
Truths: no answer without a durable receipt; result content is immutable; hints are governed and bound; previews leak no answer key/value. Artifacts: lookup pointer, attempt snapshot, projection, seed gates, and tests. Key links: challenge version to receipt, receipt to result, and approval provenance to hint.
</must_haves>

<threat_model>
ASVS L1 assets are correct answers, explanations, feedback, hint text, challenge versions, attempt receipts, and student preview schemas. Trust boundaries are seeded content to direct lookup, current challenge to immutable receipt, receipt to answer-bearing projection, and reviewer approval to pre-attempt hint. High-severity blockers are scan-first duplicate challenge resolution, answer from current edited/deleted content, ID reuse changing a result, result without durable receipt, free-form substring-only hint approval, stale approval after content edit, recursive key/value leakage, or raw private canary. Mitigate with pointer-only direct keys, version/content hashes, conditional immutable attempts, snapshot-only projections, reviewer/policy provenance, hash revalidation, fail-closed approval, and route-level recursive canary tests.
</threat_model>
