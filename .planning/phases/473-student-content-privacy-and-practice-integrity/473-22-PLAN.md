# Plan 473-22: Exhaustive retention and deletion reconciliation

<objective>
Make conversation deletion, resource release, and account purge exhaustive and crash-resumable under pagination, GSI lag, concurrent association attempts, and provider/repository lost responses, per D-07 and D-09 through D-17.

Purpose: Preserve durable attachments for exactly their authorized lifetime and prove last-reference deletion without truncation or split-brain quota state.
Output: Strong exhaustive enumeration, lifecycle fences, typed reconciliation, cleanup debt, and adversarial retention tests.
</objective>

## Artifacts this phase produces

- Wave-0 retention tests covering pagination, GSI lag, split metadata/association pages, and concurrency.
- Durable resource/account fences plus strong authoritative reference enumeration.
- Resumable provider deletion/quota/reference/promotion-debt reconciliation.

<must_haves>
Truths: every durable reference is found; no new reference races a purge; deletion/finalization can resume after every failure. Artifacts: cursor/fence contracts, typed outcomes, and stateful tests. Key links: fence to transaction, strong enumeration to release, and exact absence to quota finalization.
</must_haves>

<threat_model>
ASVS L1 assets are student attachments, associations, quota bytes, deletion fences, provider versions, account-closure completeness, and private diagnostics. Trust boundaries are eventually consistent indexes to authoritative table reads, delete requests to concurrent association transactions, provider deletion to quota finalization, and purge cursors to later invocations. High-severity blockers are single-page truncation, GSI-lag omission, split-page join loss, association after fence, reference absence inferred from eventual data, object deleted but quota/ref irrecoverably stuck, cleanup debt discarded, or raw coordinates. Mitigate with strongly consistent exhaustive scans/keys, transactional fence checks, durable cursors/quiescence, exact provider absence proof from Plan 19, typed incomplete outcomes, idempotent finalization, and redacted stateful tests.
</threat_model>
