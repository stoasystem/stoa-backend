# Plan 473-13: Gateway and replay robustness

<objective>
Close WR-006 through WR-008 in source and adversarial tests: deterministic replay attachment identity, exhaustive structured gateway dependency errors, and exact-version provider-body closure on every exit.
</objective>

## Artifacts this phase produces

- `bind_message_attachments(..., deterministic_attachment_ids=...)` with a distinct `bound_attachment_ids` output accumulator and exact fresh-ID cardinality validation.
- One exhaustive internal gateway dependency translator/helper covering initial intent lookup, chunk claim/replay polling, provider part reconciliation, part listing, assembly claim, completed-state re-read, validation/promotion recovery, and cleanup recovery.
- A provider-body lifetime helper/pattern that closes the exact S3 response body in `finally` for validation/promotion and conversation extraction reads.
- Route-level dependency injection matrices and exact deterministic attachment-key replay tests.

<must_haves>
Truths: deterministic replay identity, stable dependency errors, and provider resource closure are executable and preserve Plan 473-12 cleanup behavior. Artifacts: service fixes and adversarial source-level tests. Key links: command-derived IDs flow unchanged into persistence, all dependency failures cross one closed translation boundary, and every provider body is closed in `finally`.
</must_haves>

<threat_model>
ASVS L1 assets are durable attachment identity, opaque upload existence/state, provider connection capacity, student content, and immutable coordinates. Trust boundaries are command replay into attachment binding, repository/provider exceptions into public HTTP, and provider streaming bodies into bounded parsers. High-severity blocking threats are duplicate or non-deterministic durable effects, private state or provider diagnostics escaping through 500 responses, connection-pool exhaustion, and regression of Plan 473-12 cleanup safety; any unresolved high-severity threat blocks this plan. Mitigations are exact cardinality-checked deterministic IDs, exhaustive closed error translation with concealed conditional conflicts, `finally` body closure, and route-level injected failures. Verification includes synchronized retries, generic and typed repository failures at every gateway stage, body-close spies on every exit, retained cleanup tests, targeted Ruff, and `git diff --check`. Source locking, exhaustive D-01 through D-22 adjudication, inventory generation, and evidence publication are intentionally deferred to dependent Plan 473-14.
</threat_model>
