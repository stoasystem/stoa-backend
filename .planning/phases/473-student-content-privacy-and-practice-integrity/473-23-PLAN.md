# Plan 473-23: Saved attachment access and durable attachment-purge branch

<objective>
Deliver the owner-facing saved attachment lifecycle promised by D-10 through D-17, including coordinate-free list/view/download/delete and the reusable durable attachment-purge branch consumed by the authoritative account-deletion lifecycle in Plan 473-29.

Purpose: Let students recover storage and access their own durable files without weakening immutable ownership or account-closure deletion guarantees.
Output: Typed routes/models, exact immutable streaming, reference-safe delete, typed attachment-purge branch, and privacy tests.
</objective>

## Artifacts this phase produces

- Wave-0 API/privacy tests for owner, foreign, missing, over-quota, range/stream, and filename cases.
- Saved attachment models and `/files/attachments` routes.
- A typed resumable attachment-purge branch that owns only attachment/reference/provider debt and cannot report aggregate account closure.

<must_haves>
Truths: owners can manage saved attachments; foreign/missing are concealed; only exact immutable bytes stream; the attachment purge branch has independent durable progress and never finalizes the account. Artifacts: models, routes, service methods, typed branch result, and tests. Key links: opaque ID to authorization, immutable tuple to stream, delete to reconciliation, and account fence to the attachment branch consumed by Plan 473-29.
</must_haves>

<threat_model>
ASVS L1 assets are attachment bytes/metadata, immutable provider coordinates, owner identity, active associations, quota availability, durable purge debt, and private filenames. Trust boundaries are opaque route IDs to loaded records, provider body/headers to HTTP streaming, delete intent to retention fences, and the account fence to the attachment purge branch. High-severity blockers are foreign enumeration, download of current-key rather than exact version, coordinate/checksum disclosure, deletion of referenced content, quota blocking read/delete, Windows backslash filename leakage, attachment progress that is count-based or clears the aggregate account fence, or unclosed body. Mitigate with load-once owner authorization, allowlist models, exact version reads, body ownership, stable in-use action, Plan 22 typed deletion, normalized basename, and a typed branch result with its own durable cursor/debt/quiescence facts. Plan 473-29 alone owns aggregate question/OCR plus attachment closure.
</threat_model>
