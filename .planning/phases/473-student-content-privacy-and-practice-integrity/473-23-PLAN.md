# Plan 473-23: Saved attachment access and account-private-content purge hook

<objective>
Deliver the owner-facing saved attachment lifecycle promised by D-10 through D-17, including coordinate-free list/view/download/delete and an explicit durable account-private-content purge integration contract.

Purpose: Let students recover storage and access their own durable files without weakening immutable ownership or account-closure deletion guarantees.
Output: Typed routes/models, exact immutable streaming, reference-safe delete, purge hook, and privacy tests.
</objective>

## Artifacts this phase produces

- Wave-0 API/privacy tests for owner, foreign, missing, over-quota, range/stream, and filename cases.
- Saved attachment models and `/files/attachments` routes.
- A clearly owned account purge hook for attachment plus OCR-derived question content deletion.

<must_haves>
Truths: owners can manage saved attachments; foreign/missing are concealed; only exact immutable bytes stream; account purge has an owned durable entry point. Artifacts: models, routes, service methods, and tests. Key links: opaque ID to authorization, immutable tuple to stream, delete to reconciliation, and account lifecycle to purge hook.
</must_haves>

<threat_model>
ASVS L1 assets are attachment bytes/metadata, immutable provider coordinates, owner identity, active associations, quota availability, OCR-derived question content, and private filenames. Trust boundaries are opaque route IDs to loaded records, provider body/headers to HTTP streaming, delete intent to retention fences, and account lifecycle to purge callback. High-severity blockers are foreign enumeration, download of current-key rather than exact version, coordinate/checksum disclosure, deletion of referenced content, quota blocking read/delete, Windows backslash filename leakage, purge hook that can report success before private question content and attachment debt complete, or unclosed body. Mitigate with load-once owner authorization, allowlist models, exact version reads, body ownership, stable in-use action, Plan 22 typed deletion, normalized basename, and durable aggregate purge outcome.
</threat_model>
