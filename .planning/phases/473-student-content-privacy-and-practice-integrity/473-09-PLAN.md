# Plan 473-09: Stable transaction-cancellation taxonomy

<objective>
Close WR-003 by preserving DynamoDB transaction atomicity while mapping only known operation indices and cancellation categories to the correct stable D-16 response.
</objective>

## Artifacts this phase produces

- `AttachmentTransactionOutcome` values `concealed_resource_conflict`, `quota_exceeded`, and `retryable_dependency`.
- A `TransactionOperation` descriptor carrying semantic kind plus the DynamoDB item, so serialized and fake transactions share identical indices.
- Category-only parsing of `CancellationReasons[*].Code`; reason messages and item/key payloads are never retained or logged.
- Service mappings and zero-effect quota/dependency race matrices for question and conversation commits.

<threat_model>
TOCTOU and concurrent-write threats arise when quota or owner/status changes between preflight and commit; the transaction remains the authoritative all-or-nothing boundary. Replay threats are not resolved by guessing from cancellations; owner/status/duplicate conflicts stay concealed and Plan 473-10 adds command replay. Log-disclosure threats include Dynamo cancellation messages/items and private coordinates, all discarded before the repository exception crosses the boundary. Error-oracle threats are controlled by mapping only the specifically named quota operation to a visible quota error; resource conditions remain not-found. Quota/ref double effects are tested by snapshotting every fake row before injected cancellation and asserting an identical snapshot after. Provider failures and throttling map to one retryable category and safe 503. Immutable-byte/version behavior from Plan 473-08 is retained in every transaction fixture.
</threat_model>
