# Plan 473-05: Answer-free previews and recorded-attempt results

<objective>
Replace answer-bearing dictionaries across every student practice/curriculum read with one answer-free projection, and make standard answers/full explanations available only from a result tied to a successfully persisted student attempt.
</objective>

## Artifacts this phase produces

- `build_challenge_preview`, `build_lesson_preview`, `build_exercise_preview`, `build_attempt_result`, and `approved_directional_hint` in `practice_projection_service.py`.
- `put_attempt`, `get_attempt`, and `list_student_attempts` repository operations that record both correct and incorrect attempts with submitted answer.
- Student `POST /practice/challenges/{challenge_id}/answer` typed result and `GET /practice/attempts/{attempt_id}/result` owner-result route.
- Answer-free schemas for overview, roadmap/path, lesson and curriculum catalog/exercise routes.

<must_haves>
Truths: D-18/D-19/D-20 are invariant across legacy and curriculum route families. Artifacts: allowlist projection, durable receipt and result route. Key links: no result builder runs before a successful attempt write; no preview builder accepts an answer-bearing flag.
</must_haves>

<threat_model>
ASVS L1 assets: standard answers, explanations, answer-derived feedback, attempt ownership and mastery integrity. Trust boundaries: stored curriculum content to preview projection; student answer to comparison/write; durable attempt to result; hint content to pre-submit response. Threats: legacy route leak, nested explanation/feedback leak, `includeAnswers=true`, client-side hiding, result after failed write, guessed attempt ID, wrong student's attempt, hint equal to/deriving answer, or correct attempts not recorded. Mitigations: typed allowlist preview, recursive forbidden-key tests, remove student answer toggles, write-before-result, owner-resolved attempt route, record every attempt, conservative hint approval marker/content guard, and redacted safe errors. Verification: every route snapshot, write failure sentinels, owner/foreign attempt matrix and malicious hint fixtures. Residual risk: semantic derivability cannot be proven mechanically; only explicitly approved hints are served and unsafe legacy hints remain unavailable until reviewed.
</threat_model>
