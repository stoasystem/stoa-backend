# Plan 473-27: Systemic boundary inventory and compositional fault gate

<objective>
Create the dedicated pre-evidence systemic inventory requested for Phase 473 and make every relevant lower boundary fail closed unless registered with executable adversarial coverage, spanning D-01 through D-22.

Purpose: Prevent future lower-bound operations, partial pages, malformed successes, or transport variants from hiding behind high-level happy-path tests.
Output: Deterministic boundary and route inventories plus compositional sentinel gates.
</objective>

## Artifacts this phase produces

- A deterministic AST/source-backed inventory of every Phase 473 security/data boundary.
- One exact lower-bound selector and applicable failure-dimension declaration per boundary entry.
- Checked route inventory for all new saved attachment and retained practice routes.

<must_haves>

Artifacts: generator, checked JSON, tests, and route inventory. Key links: source operation to registry, registry to selector, selector to actual lower-bound fake.
</must_haves>

<threat_model>

Trust boundaries are source discovery to checked inventory, inventory declarations to actual tests, high-level adapters to lower provider/repository/parser clients, and routes to authorization metadata. High-severity blockers are an undiscovered/unregistered operation, selector that does not exercise its named boundary, high-level monkeypatch-only satisfaction, omitted SSE or regular transport, missing malformed/truncation/unprocessed/lost-response/redaction dimension, nondeterministic inventory, stale route inventory, or canary leakage. Mitigate with AST discovery, exact source symbol/operation IDs, selector/source validation, runtime sentinels, byte-stable generation, inventory `--check`, and fixed-string privacy denial.
</threat_model>
