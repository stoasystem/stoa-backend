# Plan 473-18: Provider acknowledgement and recovery integrity

<objective>
Close the lower provider acknowledgement and recovery gaps so only exact, ledger-proven bytes can advance from a multipart part through staging recovery and immutable promotion, per D-03 through D-09 and D-13 through D-17.

Purpose: Prevent malformed or ambiguous provider success from becoming durable student content, and make crash recovery converge without guessing.
Output: Strict provider/repository contracts, create-only promotion, and adversarial stateful tests.
</objective>

## Artifacts this phase produces

- A Wave-0 adversarial provider state-machine suite that first fails on the current coercive/first-match behavior.
- Exact ETag, checksum, count, marker, metadata, and version acknowledgement parsers.
- Ledger-bound staging recovery and create-only immutable promotion with commit-then-raise convergence.

<must_haves>
Truths: malformed acknowledgements never complete a part or clear a fence; recovery cannot select an unproven object; immutable bytes cannot be overwritten. Artifacts: strict parsers, durable invariants, and stateful tests. Key links: provider acknowledgement to ledger, ledger to recovery, and promotion to exact immutable verification.
</must_haves>

<threat_model>
ASVS L1 assets are student bytes, multipart identity, exact ETags/checksums, immutable versions, ownership, and private diagnostics. Trust boundaries are client bytes to service hashing, S3-shaped dictionaries to typed internal facts, and repository state to recovery decisions. High-severity blockers are accepting a blank/coerced ETag, non-canonical or unequal checksum, bool-as-integer, partial/ambiguous object match, overwriteable immutable promotion, coordinate removal before exact absence, or raw provider/parser detail in API/log/test evidence. Mitigate with closed shape parsers, conditional writes, ledger digests, unique-match refusal, create-only promotion, post-write verification, redacted structured codes, and stateful commit-then-raise tests. Residual real-S3 behavior remains NOT RUN for Phase 479.
</threat_model>
